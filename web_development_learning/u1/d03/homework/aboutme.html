I was born in Bangladesh. I immigrated with my family to US when I was 15 years old. Even though I started high school in the middle of freshman year, I graduated top of my class and went on to Cornell University to study Neurobiology. I contemplated going to medical school but decided to apply my science background in another industry first.

My first job after college was at a law firm analyzing scientific literature to assist in legal cases. My speciatly was helping expert witnesses testify in trials. From there I moved onto a project management role where I oversaw millions of dollars of settlements for my clients.

After a few years as a settlement manager, I decided to enrich myself with an MBA education from Kelley School of Business at Indiana University. My studies allowed me to travel to to Ghana and help a microfinance startup along with building marketing strategies for a local veterinary hospital in Bloomington, IN. For my internship, I worked at Medtronic and created a competitive landscape analysis for their marketing department.

Since graduation, I spent a year establishing a startup law firm specializing in intellectual property. Having experienced entrepreneurship first-hand and seeing opportunities in web develop industry, I decided to join a web development immersive course at General Assembly. My goal is to use my new skillset to help entrepreneurs build their businesses online and guide them challenging roads for startups.

In my leisure, I love to explore new and unique designs with friends and family. I also dedicate a portion of my time volunteering for some non-profits that seeks to improve education for the marginalized in developing countries.
